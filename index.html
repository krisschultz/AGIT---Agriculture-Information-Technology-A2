Analysis of the individual test results for a collaborative environment

The Group’s test result varied a great deal. Looking just at the Myers Brigs type indicator test results first and the splits between each indicator by team member. The group can be seen to trend towards Intuition and Feeling, while being divided relatively equally between Extraversion/Introversion and Judging/Perceiving.

Being aware of each members different personality types can be useful to the team as it shows that we are a varied group and that we should be ready for each team member to go about their work in different ways and to understand that not everyone will be able to conform to another team members view of how to complete the project.

The MBIT provides the strengths of each personality type it also provides the weaknesses of each type. We can see though that each team member’s weakness is counteracted by another team member’s strength. So, while one team member might, “find it difficult to focus”, other team members are both “tolerant” and “Loyal, Patient and Reliable”. In the same vein another team member is “Inflexible and stubborn” but others are “Curious, Imaginative and Sensitive to others”. The accuracy of the MBIT can certainly be questioned but the test serves its purpose to show that while each team member is different it’s important for each of us to appreciate that fact and not become frustrated or dejected when our differences clash.

The Group also completed Learning Style Tests, the results of these test extend the learnings from the MBIT tests. We had 3 tactile learners, 1 visual learner and 2 auditory learners. These results further highlight the need to appreciate each other’s differences but also to understand our own learning style which should help each team member approach the project tasks in a ways that optimise their contribution.

Each group member selected a third test of their own choosing 3 team members completed a Big Five Personality Test the other three team members completed a psychometric test, a team roles Test and a political compass. The varied choice of test again highlights the diversity of the group. The results of these tests themselves are less useful than the other tests as not all members completed the same test.

Comparison of our jobs & career parthes

The Team's ideal jobs are as varied as the team members themselves. 5 of the roles are IT specific with only one being finance related. There is however a great deal of alignment when looking at the general skills required for all the jobs. All the roles require Communication Skills and 5 of the 6 require Problem Solving. In general, of the skills that are required, there was very few that were not included in at least two thirds of the jobs.

The IT skills were more differentiated. Some jobs like Front end Developer and Full Stack Developer required in depth knowledge on specific programming languages and are more technical in nature. One Job was Chief Technology Officer which as the most senior of the jobs requires in depth knowledge as well and management and business skills. Two of the roles, Information Technology Support and DevOps/Agile Consultant tie together business process and Its knowledge.

The career paths of the group are generally quite different. Some of the group, like Tee and Angelo are looking to start their careers in IT and specifically in development work they have a strong passion for the technical side of IT. Ben currently works in financial advice and while he is not looking to move into working in IT he see the value of understanding IT as he moves up in his career in the finance industry. Similarly, Kristian M is looking to combine logistics and supply chain management and IT. Nathan works in finance but is looking to transition to an IT focused role which can still draw on his experience is finance and business. Kristian S is looking to build on his management and leadership experience by upskilling in IT. The end goals of each team member do vary widely but it's clear that was is similar is that each member is building on their current experience and passions to plan a career that can utilise their current skills and their passion for IT.
