Group Work Quality and Changes Made

The group has had a beneficial experience of working together and communicating with one other in Assignment 2. Despite a slow start, the communication between group members increased and improved quickly after each group meeting held. Furthermore, the workflow of the group steadily increased towards the deadline of the assignment. The progress of each member's work was communicated through a group conversation on WhatsApp, a simple means of text messaging, while the main discussions concerning the tasks were discussed and completed over voice conferences in Microsoft Teams. These communication tools combined with the mind map that held an overview of the delegated work tasks that needed to be completed, formed a strong foundation that the group could rely on over the time spent working together to complete the assignment. However, there are still many areas that can be improved on to further increase the group's productivity.

From beginning work on Assignment 3, the group has seen a radical shift in processes of group work. Firstly, the group has increased the number of group meetings held every week from once a week to twice a week. This allows the group to discuss important details together before starting on individual tasks. Additionally, the group has introduced more flexible meeting times. As a result of our increased communication, the group brings forward or postpones meetings to best suit the availability of every member. In the case of group members still being unable to participate, the group meetings for Assignment 3 are now recorded for those who are absent so that they can be kept up to date.

Moreover, as the group continues to gain familiarity with Microsoft Teams, the group has begun using more of its functionalities more efficiently. For example, the group now organises the files stored in Microsoft Team's cloud storage system. This is because of the large number of files required as a part of the group's various ongoing projects. The group has also started using a task plan with multiple columns and checkboxes that delegate the group work instead of relying on the mind map as previously when the group worked on Assignment 2. This gives the group a more structured and organised outlook on the work to be completed and by whom. It also provides a clear and detailed manner of viewing internal deadlines and allows group members to comment on each task and post relevant details and opinions.
